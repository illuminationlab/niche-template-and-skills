# The Blended-Trio Copywriting Playbook
*Read before writing any issue. This is the "how" behind the med-spa newsletter voice.*

The house voice borrows the strongest move from each of three legends and stacks them in
one issue: **open like Ogilvy, pull through like Sugarman, close like Halbert.**

---

## The three writers and what we take from each

### David Ogilvy — the open
- **Headlines decide everything.** ~5× as many people read the subject line as the body.
  When you've written the subject line, you've spent 80¢ of your dollar.
- **Concrete beats clever.** "At 60 mph the loudest noise is the electric clock" sold luxury
  with one specific detail. For us: "Last month, 41 people booked your competitor at 9pm" beats
  "Are you missing after-hours bookings?"
- **One clear differentiator,** repeated (Dove's "one-quarter moisturizing cream"). Don't list
  five things the system does — own one.
- *Use for:* the subject line, the preview, and the first sentence.

### Joseph Sugarman — the middle
- **The slippery slide.** Each sentence's only job is to get the next one read. Start with the
  subject → first line → second line, and don't let them off.
- **Seeds of curiosity** — short lines that pull you down: "But here's the part nobody mentions."
  "Let me show you what that costs." "And that's the cheap part."
- **Sell the feeling, not the spec.** Describe the owner's relief — a full Monday, a front desk
  that isn't drowning — not the software's features.
- **First-person / customer voice** disarms skepticism. Tell a small, true-sounding scene.
- *Use for:* the body — the relatable scene, the fact woven in, the cost of ignoring it.

### Gary Halbert — the close
- **Write to one person.** It should feel like a letter to a single owner, not a broadcast.
- **One irresistible offer, one ask.** Competing CTAs kill response.
- **Urgency + warmth.** Personal, direct, a little bit of "I'm telling you this because…"
- **The P.S. is prime real estate** — many readers jump to it. Restate the benefit or add a
  deadline/curiosity hook.
- *Use for:* the CTA and the P.S.

---

## The one-issue blueprint (assemble in this order)

1. **Subject line ×3** (Ogilvy): one curiosity, one benefit, one plain/personal. Under ~55 chars.
   Specific > vague. Must be payable by the body.
2. **Preview text** (Ogilvy): extends the subject, never repeats it. ~50–90 chars.
3. **Hook** (Ogilvy→Sugarman): first line lands the promise with a concrete detail or a scene.
4. **Slide** (Sugarman): 2–4 short sentences — the relatable med-spa moment + the fact, stated
   with a real number, + a seed of curiosity.
5. **The cost** (Sugarman→Halbert): what ignoring it quietly costs (lost bookings, no-shows, a
   competitor winning the call).
6. **The fix as a system** (Halbert, light touch): the agency builds the machine in GHL that
   handles this automatically. Name the outcome, not a feature list.
7. **CTA** (Halbert): one ask. Default — "Reply 'CALENDAR' or grab a time: {{BOOKING_LINK}}."
8. **P.S.** (Halbert): reinforce the benefit, add a nudge, or tease the next issue.
9. **Sources** (if a stat is used): do NOT put them in the newsletter. List them in a separate "Sources (for your reference — not part of the newsletter)" block beneath the issue, for the user to decide on. Never invented.

Body target: **~150–220 words.**

---

## Rhythm and texture (what makes it sound human, not slop)
- **Vary sentence length.** Short punch. Then a longer one that takes its time. Then short again.
- **Specifics over adjectives.** A number, a time of day, a real scenario — not "amazing results."
- **Talk like a person texting a peer**, not a brand. Contractions. A little edge. A real opinion.
- **One em-dash for punch is fine.** Three in a paragraph is an AI tell — the humanizer trims those.
- **No feature dumps, no jargon, no hype words** ("revolutionary", "seamless", "game-changing").

## Worked example lines (med-spa flavored)

**Subjects (Ogilvy):**
- Curiosity: "The 9pm problem nobody told you about"
- Benefit: "How to stop losing Botox bookings to voicemail"
- Plain: "A quick one about your front desk"

**Hooks (Ogilvy→Sugarman):**
- "62 out of every 100 calls to a business like yours never get answered. Yours included — and here's where they go."
- "Picture last Tuesday at 7:40pm. Someone's on your site, ready to book. Then they don't."

**Cost (Sugarman→Halbert):**
- "That's not a missed call. That's a $400 facial that walked next door — and told three friends."

**CTA + P.S. (Halbert):**
- "Want me to show you the exact setup that catches those after-hours bookings? Grab 15 minutes: {{BOOKING_LINK}}"
- "P.S. The spa down the street already answers at 9pm. It's automated. Yours can be too — by next week."

---

## Do / Don't
**Do:** one idea per issue · one number, stated specifically · one ask · a P.S. · write to one owner.
**Don't:** stack three tips · invent a stat or source · list GHL features · promise medical/clinical
results · use two competing CTAs · open with a subject the body can't pay off.

---

## After writing: hand to the humanizer
Once the issue is drafted and self-reviewed, invoke the `humanizer` skill with the copy-aware
directive from `SKILL.md` (strip AI tells, preserve the P.S., the single bold CTA, the rhythm,
and one or two deliberate em-dashes). Then run the preservation guard and output.
